# Arbitration Session 04: Web3 & Token Economy Projections

**Date:** February 2026
**Topic:** Sizing the Web3 layer (Tokenized Loyalty, TGE, and On-chain Infrastructure).
**Participants:**
- **Agent Web3:** Advocate for CheckIn’s decentralized infrastructure potential.
- **Methodological Conservative Bot (MCB):** VC-aligned skeptic enforcing strict sizing rules regarding crypto/blockchain models.

---

**Agent Web3:** Our Tokenized Loyalty potential is massive. The APAC Tokenized Loyalty point market alone is $2.5B, growing at 27.5% CAGR. Global Web3 loyalty platforms represent a $1.86B software market in 2025. Given CheckIn's dual-credit economy (Global Credits & Venue Credits), migrating this to an on-chain ledger makes CheckIn a dominant player in this space. I propose we add the full $2.5B APAC Tokenized Loyalty market as our Web3 TAM.

**MCB:** *[Flagged]* Dangerous territory. You cannot claim the entire APAC Tokenized Loyalty market just because you intend to put your credits on a blockchain. The $2.5B figure includes airline miles, massive retail conglomerates, and existing enterprise solutions. What portion of this is realistically addressable by CheckIn's specific network (India + SEA physical venues)? Furthermore, VCs deeply discount non-revenue "crypto TAM." If you don't show revenue mechanics, it's just buzzwords.

**Agent Web3:** Accepted. Let's restrict the Web3 TAM purely to revenue mechanisms CheckIn can directly monetize.

1. **The Infrastructure Layer (Enterprise Nodes):** Right now, large enterprise chains (like a massive retail conglomerate) want to tokenize their loyalty but lack the infrastructure. CheckIn provides it. They launch their "Venue Credits" on CheckIn's L2 subnet. They pay CheckIn an enterprise SaaS fee + transaction gas fees. 
   - Top 500 retail/hospitality chains in India/SEA.
   - Average infrastructure + transaction volume cost: $5,000/month per enterprise.
   - $5,000 × 500 enterprises = $2.5M/mo = **$30 Million TAM.**

2. **The TGE & Secondary Market (Token Velocity):** We launch the CKIN token (governance, premium entry, brand staking). A realistic seed/Series-A TGE in Singapore/Dubai raises $10M-$15M. That's a capital event, not recurring revenue. But once the token is trading, CheckIn takes a tiny transaction fee (say 0.5%) on the swapping of Global Credits into Venue Credits or fiat off-ramps via liquidity pools. 
   - If CheckIn’s network reaches $100M in annual tokenized transaction volume (comparable to mid-tier fan tokens or gaming ecosystems), at a 0.5% fee, the TAM is **$500,000**. 

**MCB:** *[Analyzing]* Very interesting. Your bottom-up revenue metrics for the Web3 layer are miniscule ($30.5M) compared to your multi-billion dollar claims in the previous prompt. Why do you insist the TAM is $2.5B?

**Agent Web3:** Ah, because the $2.5B APAC market figure represents the *Gross Network Value* (GNV) of the tokens, not CheckIn's SaaS revenue. This is a common pitfall. A crypto exchange doesn't claim the market cap of Bitcoin as its revenue TAM; it claims the trading fees. 

However, VCs assessing a Web3 startup *do* value the Gross Network Value capability. If CheckIn facilitates $1B worth of on-chain loyalty points exchanging hands, the *platform valuation* scales with the GNV, even if the direct revenue take-rate is small. 

To be ruthlessly conservative and strictly methodological:
- **Revenue TAM:** $30.5M (Enterprise Infra + Swap Fees).
- **Capital Event (SOM):** $15M TGE Event.
- **Narrative TAM (Addressable GNV):** $1 Billion-2.5 Billion (The ceiling of the token economy CheckIn aims to facilitate in APAC).

**MCB:** This distinction is saving your credibility. If you pitch a "$2.5B Web3 TAM" to a traditional SaaS VC, they will laugh you out of the room when they realize it's GNV, not Revenue. 

You must separate them:
1. **Core SaaS/Payments TAM:** $4.8 Billion (From Sessions 1, 2, 3).
2. **Web3 GNV (Ecosystem Valuation Ceiling):** $2.5 Billion.
3. **Web3 Direct Revenue TAM:** $30 Million.

**Agent Web3:** Agreed. The Web3 layer is primarily an infrastructure network effect mechanism and a capital generation event (TGE), not the primary software revenue driver in the TAM model. 

**MCB:** *[Validating]* I am accepting this. Web3 Direct Revenue TAM ($30M) is added to the B2B TAM stack. The $2.5B figure is strictly categorized as "Addressable Token Network Value (GNV)" to illustrate upside, but it is intentionally firewalled from the traditional SaaS revenue calculations to maintain methodological integrity.
