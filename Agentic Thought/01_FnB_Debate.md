# Arbitration Session 01: F&B Venue Market Sizing & ARPU

**Date:** February 2026
**Topic:** Establishing the baseline TAM and SAM for the F&B beachhead market.
**Participants:**
- **Agent F&B:** Advocate for the F&B venue market potential.
- **Methodological Conservative Bot (MCB):** VC-aligned skeptic enforcing strict sizing rules.

---

**Agent F&B:** Let's establish the baseline. India has roughly 1.2 million organized and semi-organized F&B venues. I propose we use an ARPU of $134/month based on our Standard Tier, yielding a TAM of ~$1.9 Billion. For SAM, we look at the top 20 metros, which gives us about 350,000 qualifying venues. That's a SAM of ~$566M.

**Methodological Conservative Bot (MCB):** *[Flagged]* Methodological error detected. You cannot size a TAM purely on what you *hope* to charge ($134/mo). A true TAM measures the current "Addressable Wallet"—what these venues are already spending on the problem you solve. Furthermore, including 400,000 "semi-organized" venues in your baseline assumes they have the digital maturity to adopt an AI engagement platform. 

**Agent F&B:** Understood, let's recalibrate to Addressable Wallet. Venues currently pay 15-28% to aggregators, plus marketing and CRM tools like Reelo. A mid-range venue easily spends $300-$500/month on customer acquisition and retention. If we use the lower bound ($300/mo) for the 800,000 strictly *organized* venues, the F&B addressable wallet is **$2.88 Billion**. 

**MCB:** That is a much more defensible methodology. You are sizing the existing spend, not theoretical pricing power. However, your SAM calculation is still flawed. You claim 350,000 qualifying venues in the top 20 metros. Are all of them generating enough revenue to care about advanced engagement tools?

**Agent F&B:** Fair pushback. Let's filter the SAM strictly. We only want venues in the top 20 metros that have an average bill value >₹500 and use a modern POS system. Based on NRAI data, roughly 60% of organized metro venues fit this profile. 
- Top 6 Metros (Tier 1): ~240,000 venues. 60% qualifier = 144,000.
- Next 14 Metros (Tier 2): ~112,000 venues. 60% qualifier = 67,200.
- Revised SAM Venue Count: 211,200 venues.
If we apply our target Standard Tier ARPU ($134/month) strictly to this highly qualified SAM, the SAM is **$339 Million**.

**MCB:** *[Validating]* Let's review the logic. 
1. **TAM:** $2.88B based on $300/mo existing wallet spend across 800K strictly organized venues. *Methodologically sound.*
2. **SAM:** 211,200 highly-qualified, POS-integrated metro venues at $134/mo projected ARPU = $339M. *Methodologically sound.*

**Agent F&B:** To expand on this, if we capture just Bengaluru's 2,500 premium Gen-Z venues in Year 1-2 (our SOM) at the initial $97/mo blended ARPU, that's a **$2.9 Million** baseline SaaS/Agentic recurring revenue, ignoring all consumer/upside layers.

**MCB:** Accepted. The F&B baseline is cleared. $2.88B TAM, $339M SAM. This is conservative because it completely removes the 400K semi-organized venues and strictly filters the metro venues, but it remains large enough to justify the beachhead.
