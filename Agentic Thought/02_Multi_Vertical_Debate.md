# Arbitration Session 02: Multi-Vertical Expansion Valuation

**Date:** February 2026
**Topic:** Sizing the expansion into Retail, Wellness, Coworking, and Events.
**Participants:**
- **Agent Multi-Vertical:** Advocate for the platform scalability into non-F&B verticals.
- **Methodological Conservative Bot (MCB):** VC-aligned skeptic enforcing strict sizing rules.

---

**Agent Multi-Vertical:** CheckIn is built on an agentic architecture. The quests and credit systems are vertical-agnostic. We should project TAM for Retail, Gyms/Wellness, Coworking, and Bars/Lounges. India has 12 million retail outlets, of which roughly 8% (960,000) are organized. Add gyms and salons, and we easily have 1.5 million additional target venues. At an ARPU of $70/month, the Multi-Vertical TAM adds **$1.26 Billion**.

**MCB:** *[Flagged]* Two severe methodological errors. First, you are sizing TAM based on your theoretical ARPU again ($70/mo), ignoring the precedent we just set in Session 01. Second, you are assuming a gym requires the same engagement frequency as a café. A cafe has 4-8 visits a month. A retail fashion brand might see a customer 3 times a *year*. The engagement software value prop is different. You cannot blanket-apply identical logic.

**Agent Multi-Vertical:** Acknowledged. We must correct for frequency and addressable wallet. 
Let's break down the wallet by vertical based on *their* specific retention spend:
1. **Organized Retail (Fashion, D2C, Electronics):** ~800,000 high-tier outlets. Their loyalty problem is acute (high customer acquisition cost). Current spend on CRM/Loyalty points is easily ~$150/month per outlet. **TAM = $1.44 Billion.**
2. **Wellness & Fitness (Gyms, Spas):** ~100,000 premium facilities. Churn is their biggest enemy. They currently spend on membership management and retention software. Addressable wallet: ~$100/month. **TAM = $120 Million.**
3. **Bars/Lounges & Coworking:** ~70,000 venues. High ticket sizes, massive emphasis on community. Addressable wallet: ~$200/month. **TAM = $168 Million.**

**Total Multi-Vertical Addressable Wallet (TAM): $1.72 Billion.**

**MCB:** The segmentation is better. But for the SAM, you must be ruthlessly realistic. When will CheckIn actually expand outside F&B? You won't touch retail until Year 3.

**Agent Multi-Vertical:** Correct. So for the SAM, we only count the *immediate* adjacent verticals in the top 20 metros that share the exact same consumer base as our F&B users: Fitness, Bars/Lounges, and Coworking. 
- Bars/Lounges (Metro): 40,000
- Premium Gyms/Spas (Metro): 30,000
- Coworking (Metro): 3,000
- **Total Adjacent SAM Venues = 73,000.**
Applying our projected CheckIn ARPU for these segments ($80/mo blended), the **Adjacent SAM is $70 Million.**

Organized Retail is kept entirely in the TAM for now, reflecting a Phase 3/4 expansion.

**MCB:** *[Validating]* 
1. **TAM:** $1.72B based on segmented, vertical-specific addressable wallet estimates. *Methodologically sound.*
2. **SAM:** $70M restricted strictly to immediate lifestyle adjacencies (Gyms, Bars, Coworking) in metros, excluding general retail from the early-stage target market. *Methodologically sound and highly conservative.*

**Agent Multi-Vertical:** So grouped together, the Venue Software TAM (F&B + Multi-Vertical) is **$4.6 Billion**, with a highly qualified SAM of **$409 Million** ($339M + $70M).

**MCB:** Accepted. You have successfully decoupled the long-term platform vision (TAM) from the immediate strategic focus (SAM).
